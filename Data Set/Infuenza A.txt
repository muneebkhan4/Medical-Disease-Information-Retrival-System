Disease Name: Influenza A
Prevalence: Influenza A viruses can be transmitted through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes, and by touching contaminated surfaces.
Symptoms: Fever, cough, sore throat, runny or stuffy nose, body aches, and fatigue. In severe cases, it can lead to pneumonia and death.
Diagnosis: Rapid antigen tests that detect the presence of influenza virus in respiratory secretions or PCR tests that detect viral genetic material.
Treatments: Antiviral medications such as oseltamivir and zanamivir, and supportive care such as rest and hydration.
Preventive Measures: Annual vaccination, good hygiene practices such as washing hands frequently and avoiding close contact with sick people, and social distancing measures during flu season.
